Military Expenditure

A military budget (or military expenditure), also known as a defense budget, is the amount of financial resources dedicated by a state to raising and maintaining an armed forces or other methods essential for defense purposes. By visualizing military expenditure of top 10 spenders, which have a significant effect on international relation , we check how the arms race is going, which causes serious tension between countries.

MIDs and Countries at Wars

The definition of militarized interstate disputes (MIDs) are conflicts between states that do not involve a full-scale war. A conflict is described as an MID if it causes fewer than 1,000 deaths, and some military force is used. In the Military expenditure section, after helping people understand the trend of the figures of military expenditure, in this MIDs and War section, we want to show how the entire trend of MIDs and Wars are going by year. This information is provided with each conflict location information and countries at wars in each year on the map.

International Trade

International trade is the exchange of capital, goods, and services across international borders or territories. Many experts have couducted research on relationship between international trade and wars, because various scholar groups in international relations have argued the effect of international trade on the trend of war outbreaks. In the second section, we provide international trade information with MIDs and wars. In the last section, we will see the trend between the number of countries participating at wars and the total amount of international trade.

3. International trade has effects on preventing countries from participating at wars?

These days, international trade has significant effects leading country's economy. Therefore, many countries have developed their economy using international trade. And through international trade, interdependency on economy between countries has been increased. In many cases, the developed countries hesitate to outbreak wars or participate at war because it causes many negative effects on their countries. For example, they should spend huge amount of money winning the war instead of develping their economy. In addition, the degree of social insecurity increases dramatically. Therefore, These days, it is an unreasonable decision for many developed countries. In addition, the role of international organizations like UN is considered as one of the important factors to deter wars.

CINC score and Total Trade Value

The Composite Index of National Capability (CINC) is a statistical measure of national power created by J. David Singer for the Correlates of War project in 1963. It uses an average of percentages of world totals in six different components. The components represent demographic, economic, and military strength.[1] More recent studies tend to use the (CINC) score, which “focuses on measures that are more salient to the perception of true state power” beyond GDP. It is still “among the best-known and most accepted methods for measuring national capabilities.
